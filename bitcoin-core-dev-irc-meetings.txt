Bitcoin Core Dev IRC Meetings
-----------------------------

These meetings take place on irc.libera.chat:

Website: https://libera.chat

Webchat: https://kiwiirc.com/nextclient/irc.libera.chat

-----

*General* meeting every Thursday 1400 UTC on #bitcoin-core-dev

  General meeting logs:

  - https://bitcoin-irc.chaincode.com/bitcoin-core-dev/
  - https://www.erisian.com.au/bitcoin-core-dev/
  - https://gnusha.org/bitcoin-core-dev/

  To link to a specific line in the log history on www.erisian.com,
  append #l-<line-number> as follows:
  http://www.erisian.com.au/bitcoin-core-dev/log-2019-04-16.html#l-129

  Past and future meeting topics:
  http://gnusha.org/bitcoin-core-dev/proposedmeetingtopics.txt

  To propose a meeting topic, post this message on #bitcoin-core-dev:
  #proposedmeetingtopic <topic>

  For more information, see also:
  - https://github.com/bitcoin-core/bitcoin-devwiki/wiki/General-IRC-meeting
  - https://github.com/bitcoin-core/bitcoin-devwiki/wiki/IRC-Meeting-Cheat-Sheet

-----

*Wallet* meeting every other Friday 1900 UTC on #bitcoin-core-dev since 29 Oct 2018
(Edit: the wallet meetings are currently no longer being held.)

  Wallet meeting logs (same links as for the General meetings):

  - https://bitcoin-irc.chaincode.com/bitcoin-core-dev/
  - https://www.erisian.com.au/bitcoin-core-dev/
  - https://gnusha.org/bitcoin-core-dev/

  Past and future meeting topics:
  http://gnusha.org/bitcoin-core-dev/proposedwalletmeetingtopics.txt

  To propose a meeting topic, post this message on #bitcoin-core-dev:
  #proposedwalletmeetingtopic <topic>

-----

*PR Review Club* meeting every first Wednesday and Thursday of the month at
17:00 UTC on #bitcoin-core-pr-reviews IRC.

  Website for details:
  https://bitcoincore.reviews

  PR Review Club meeting logs are available on the website by clicking on the
  past meeting links.

  To suggest a PR or offer to host a meeting, leave a message in the IRC channel.

-----

Commands to host a #bitcoin-core-dev meeting:

  #startmeeting
  #endmeeting
  #action
  #agreed
  #help
  #info
  #idea
  #link
  #topic

  The main commands used are #startmeeting, #topic, #action, and #endmeeting.

-----

We start Bitcoin Core IRC meetings with a 'hi' so it's clear who is present.
Feel free to say hi!

Note that saying "hi" or posting on the channel at any time during a
#bitcoin-core-dev meeting adds you to archived meeting log and minutes.
